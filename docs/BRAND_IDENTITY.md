# PROSTARS™ — Brand Identity

> Companion to `PROSTARS_MASTER_PLAN.md`. This document exists to make sure PROSTARS never accidentally becomes a re-skin of the `iamstiaan/Proteus` prototype (generic lion mascot, six-sport menu, merch-store energy) or of Playball. Every choice below is made against that test: *does this feel like Elanza, or does it feel like a template?*

---

## Mission
To give every Canterbury child visible, provable proof that they are becoming more capable, more resilient, and more okay — one session at a time.

## Vision
PROSTARS becomes the name Canterbury parents reach for the way they reach for Montessori or Kindygym — a recognised category of childhood development, not a sports class with a mascot.

## Values
1. **Presence over performance.** A child who shows up regulated and tries is a win, regardless of the scoreboard.
2. **Progress you can see.** Every session produces something a parent can point to.
3. **No child left behind the room's pace.** Readiness is individual; the method adapts to the child, not the other way round.
4. **Adults earn trust by showing their work.** Plain-language updates, not jargon, not silence.
5. **Less paperwork, more presence.** A direct rejection of the bureaucracy Elanza experienced inside a big franchise — process exists to serve the child in the room, not the other way round.

## Brand Personality
Warm, direct, unimpressed by fluff, calmly confident, a little cheeky (in the way a good primary teacher is), fiercely protective of children's dignity. Speaks to parents like a trusted friend with a teaching degree, not like a corporate brochure. Never clinical, never twee.

**Brand is / brand is not:**
| PROSTARS is | PROSTARS is not |
|---|---|
| A teacher's authority, worn lightly | A drill sergeant / competitive sports academy |
| Evidence-based, plain-spoken | Clinical, diagnostic, or therapy-branded |
| Locally rooted (Canterbury-first) | A faceless national franchise |
| Playful in delivery | Twee, cartoonish, or over-branded with mascots |
| Visibly structured (the Method) | A loose "fun class" with no throughline |

## Tagline Options
1. **"Every Star Starts Somewhere."** — primary recommendation. Honest about process, not just outcome; works for a 4-year-old and a 12-year-old.
2. **"Ready, Inside and Out."** — most directly encodes the inner/outer layer of the Method; best for B2B/school-partnership materials where the pedagogy needs to land fast.
3. **"Small Steps. Real Stars."** — most parent-facing/emotional; strongest for social media and enrolment marketing.
4. "Where Readiness Begins." — category-claim register, closest to Stiaan's original "Inner Readiness" positioning; use for PR/thought-leadership, not on merchandise.

**Recommendation:** lead with *"Every Star Starts Somewhere"* publicly; keep *"Ready, Inside and Out"* as the line used with schools and in the Method explainer, where the two-layer structure needs to land quickly.

## Colour Direction

Deliberately **not** the primary-colour, six-sport-icon palette of the Proteus prototype or the red/yellow/blue register most sports franchises (Playball included) default to. PROSTARS should read as calmer and more considered — closer to a modern early-learning brand than a sports club.

| Role | Colour | Hex (starting point) | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Dusk Indigo | `#3B3A6E` | Trust, calm, "settled nervous system" — anchors the inner layer |
| Secondary | Sunrise Coral | `#FF7A59` | Warmth, energy, celebration — carries the outer layer (achievement moments) |
| Accent | Meadow Green | `#4CAF7D` | Growth, Canterbury/outdoors, "go" cue |
| Accent | Golden Star | `#F4B740` | The literal "star" motif — used sparingly, for badges/achievement only, never as a base colour |
| Neutral | Warm Sand | `#F6F1E7` | Backgrounds — soft, not clinical white, not primary-colour loud |
| Ink | Charcoal Plum | `#2B2233` | Body text — softer than pure black |

Rule of thumb: **indigo and sand carry the brand's calm; coral and gold are earned, not default** — they appear when a child achieves something, not as decoration. This is a colour system with the same inner/outer logic as the Method itself.

## Logo Concepts

1. **The Open Star** (recommended direction) — a five-point star drawn with one point deliberately open/unclosed, like a constellation still forming. Reads immediately as "star" for the brand name, but the open point encodes "in progress, not finished" — a child mid-development, not a trophy. Works at favicon size.
2. **The Pathway Star** — a star made from a single continuous line that also traces a simple path/footprint motif underneath it, literally showing "steps to becoming a star." More illustrative, better for the website hero than for a small badge.
3. **The Initial Mark** — a monogram "PS" built from two overlapping simple shapes (a rising arc for "progress" + a dot for "presence/readiness"). Most versatile for merchandise and social avatars; least literal, so it ages better if the brand expands beyond Canterbury.

**Recommendation:** Open Star as the primary mark, Initial Mark as the compact/social-avatar variant. Avoid a mascot character as the *primary* logo — see below.

## On Mascots
The Proteus prototype leans on a lion mascot in the tradition of most children's sports franchises (Playball included). PROSTARS should resist a single default-cute-animal mascot as its core identity — it's the fastest way to look like every other kids' brand and undercuts the "this is different" positioning.

Instead: a **rotating cast of small "Star Guide" characters**, one loosely tied to each inner-layer trait (a turtle for Presence/settling, a rubber-ball character for Resilience/bouncing back, an owl for Observation, etc.) used only in the Phase 2/3 digital product for badges and app moments — never as the face of the brand on signage or the logo. This keeps the adult-facing brand (website, school proposals, signage) credible and calm, while giving the child-facing product (badges, app) something playful without diluting the core mark.

## Visual Language
- **Photography over illustration** wherever possible — real Canterbury kids, real sessions, natural light, candid over posed. This is the single fastest way to differentiate from template sports-franchise stock photography.
- **Typography:** a warm, rounded-but-legible sans serif for headings (e.g. Quicksand, Poppins, or similar humanist sans) paired with a highly legible workhorse sans for body copy (e.g. Inter, Karla) — friendly without tipping into "kids' menu" typography.
- **Iconography:** simple, single-line/duotone icons for the Method's eight traits — consistent weight, never emoji-based (a deliberate contrast with the Proteus prototype's emoji-heavy page).
- **Imagery don'ts:** no stock clip-art children, no generic trophy/medal iconography as a default motif (reserve gold/star motifs for genuine achievement moments only, per the colour system above), no lion/mascot-forward hero images.
- **Tone of voice:** short sentences, second person ("you'll see..."), specific over vague ("she waited her turn three times" beats "great progress today!").

## Naming Note (for the record)
The brand went through **Proteus → ProKids → PROSTARS** during the original 2026 strategy conversation between Elanza and Stiaan. PROSTARS is the working name for this deliverable and is recommended as final: it's ownable, it spells cleanly into the two-layer Method framework above (see `PROSTARS_MASTER_PLAN.md` §4), and — unlike "Proteus" — it needs no explanation to a parent standing at a school gate.
