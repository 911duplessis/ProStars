# PROSTARS™ — 90-Day Execution Plan

> This plan is deliberately small. The original 2026 strategy work stalled for months — not because the idea was wrong, but because it asked Elanza to absorb dense documents on top of an already "sooo besig" (so busy) life running EM Muller Services and raising Mila and Lienka. Every task below is sized to fit inside real weeks, not idealised ones. If a week gets missed, the plan should bend, not break.

**Starting assumption:** ~3–5 hours/week of Elanza's time, in short blocks, not a full day set aside.

---

## Days 1–14 — Foundations (paperwork, not building)
- [ ] Confirm/renew First Aid certificate (NZ) — the actual first domino; nothing else can legally start without it.
- [ ] Confirm police check is current (NZ working-with-children requirement).
- [ ] Pick the final name and lock it: **PROSTARS** (see `BRAND_IDENTITY.md` — recommendation is to stop relitigating Proteus/ProKids and commit).
- [ ] Buy ~$80 of basic gear (mats, cones, simple props) — enough for one class, not a full kit.
- [ ] Register the business name / confirm structure (can likely sit alongside or mirror however EM Muller Services is structured, since Elanza has already done this once).

## Days 15–30 — The One Relationship That Matters
- [ ] Identify 2–3 Canterbury primary schools where Elanza already has some connection (through her daughters, community, or EM Muller Services network) — warm introductions beat cold outreach.
- [ ] Approach **one** school principal with a simple, specific ask: a hall or outdoor space for a weekly after-school slot, one term, one age band. Mention Sport Canterbury's Tū Manawa Active Aotearoa / Youth Activation Fund as context that outside providers are actively supported, not a cold pitch.
- [ ] Get to yes on one venue. This single relationship is the actual MVP — everything else in this plan is downstream of it.

## Days 31–45 — First Cohort
- [ ] Set the offer: 4–8 age band, one weekly session, term-length booking, $22–25/session, capped at 12–15 children.
- [ ] Build a one-page booking flow with tools Elanza already knows (a simple form + bank transfer or a Stripe payment link) — no custom website yet.
- [ ] Recruit the first cohort through the warmest possible channels: the partner school's existing families, Elanza's own network, EM Muller Services' existing customer goodwill, and a simple local Facebook community post.
- [ ] Set up one private parent WhatsApp/Facebook group for the cohort — this is the Phase 1 "parent community" and progress-update channel (see `PROSTARS_MASTER_PLAN.md` §5).

## Days 46–75 — Run the Method, Prove the Proof Layer
- [ ] Deliver the first 4–6 weekly sessions using the PROSTARS METHOD structure (`PROSTARS_MASTER_PLAN.md` §4): each session names one inner-layer focus and produces one outer-layer proof point.
- [ ] Send one short, specific update per child per week to their parent (a sentence, not a report) — this is the habit that later becomes the Phase 2 dashboard feature, proven manually first.
- [ ] Take photos/video each session (with consent) — this becomes both marketing material and the seed of the Phase 2 gallery feature.
- [ ] Mid-point check-in with the school principal: share what's working, keep the relationship warm for term renewal.

## Days 76–90 — Decide the Next Ninety
- [ ] Collect informal feedback from parents: are they seeing what was promised — a child who feels more capable, session to session?
- [ ] Confirm term-2 re-enrolment from the first cohort — retention, not sign-ups, is the real signal.
- [ ] Decide, based on real data rather than a hypothetical: is this Option A, B, or C energy right now? (No wrong answer — see `PROSTARS_MASTER_PLAN.md` §8. The point of the first 90 days is to make this a real choice instead of a paper one.)
- [ ] If retention and energy are strong: scope a second weekly class (new school, or a second age-band slot at the same school) and revisit `WEBSITE_BLUEPRINT.md` for when Phase 2 becomes worth building.
- [ ] If bandwidth is the constraint (the most likely outcome, given the pattern in the original 2026 conversation): it's fine to stay at one class for another term. A single retained, paying cohort is already more progress than the eight months the idea spent stalled in 2026.

---

## The Four Questions From the Original Strategy Work (still the right ones to answer honestly at Day 90)
1. Can she commit ~5–6 hours/week during school terms going forward?
2. Is she willing to keep the school-principal relationship active for renewal, not just the initial yes?
3. Is she okay staying at Option A scale without needing to decide on Option C yet?
4. Does the Inner Readiness / PROSTARS METHOD positioning feel like something she can speak to confidently from her own years of hands-on experience with children's development?

If the answer to all four is yes at Day 90, everything in this plan set — brand, website, app, franchise — is ready to build on top of a real, proven, paying local business, not a hypothesis.
