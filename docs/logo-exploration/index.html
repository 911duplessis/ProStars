PROSTARS™ — Logo Exploration, inside Star Adventure

Five ways to draw one idea

The direction is locked. This is not five brands — it's five executions of the same job: a star that begins open and becomes whole. Every mark below has to work on a website, a child's shirt, a medal, and a certificate.

1The Growing Star Recommended — primary mark

The open star, exactly as already in use. One point left undrawn — the logo is the idea: every child is a star still becoming. Powers the whole Star Journey system without needing a second mark invented for it.

Medal
Certificate

2Pip the Star Mascot

Pip's own face, set directly inside the open star outline — logo and mascot merged into one compact mark. Best as a secondary icon (app icon, favicon, social avatar) where warmth needs to read at a small size, not as the primary formal mark.

Shirt
Medal
Certificate

3Star + Path

A small trail of steps rising to a star. Reads as "journey" instantly, which makes it a strong fit for progress charts and certificates later — but it's a weaker standalone logo, since a path-plus-star needs room to breathe and gets muddy at badge size.

Shirt
Medal
Certificate

4Premium Education Mark

A thinner, fully closed, single-colour star — no mascot warmth, no gold. The direction to keep in reserve specifically for school-partnership materials and any future franchise paperwork, where "premium and credible" needs to outrank "warm and playful" for a moment.

Shirt
Medal
Certificate

5Badge Star Recommended — medal-specific mark

The star set inside a scalloped badge edge — reads as "medal" the instant you see it. Already effectively how the PROSTAR-tier medal works in the Star Journey system. Too heavy for everyday use (website, pamphlet), but exactly right anywhere something is being awarded.
