# PROSTARS™ — Master Plan

**A children's development company for Canterbury, New Zealand.**
Founder: Elanza Muller. Prepared: July 2026.

## 0. Where this comes from

This plan is not a cold-start. It is built on:

1. A full strategic research arc Elanza's brother, Stiaan du Plessis, ran with her over WhatsApp between May and June 2026 — including the "Inner Readiness" category concept, Canterbury market signals, and pricing logic (archived in `911duplessis/Mullers/03_PROKIDS`).
2. A first prototype ("Proteus Kids Sports") that Stiaan built quickly in May 2026 to give the idea a visual home while they talked (`iamstiaan/Proteus`, live at iamstiaan.github.io/Proteus). That prototype is a generic six-sport franchise site with a lion mascot and a merch store — useful as a proof that *something* could be built fast, but it is not the brand. It never answers "why this and not Playball."
3. Elanza's own lived experience as a Playball instructor, which is where the real differentiation comes from — not from a mascot, from a frustration.

The founder never said yes to a name or a direction in that first round. This document picks the thread back up, resolves the open questions Stiaan left her with, and gives PROSTARS a spine that the Proteus prototype didn't have.

## 1. Founder Story

Elanza Muller has spent her career on the floor with children, not behind a desk designing programmes for them. She holds a teaching degree and has coached inside a national children's sports franchise — Playball — where she saw, up close, both what works and what quietly doesn't.

What works: parents will show up, pay, and keep coming back for a class that makes their child visibly more confident, more coordinated, and happier at school pickup. That part of the model isn't broken.

What doesn't: the machinery underneath it. Instructors inside a big franchise are handed a script, a compliance checklist, and a territory — and very little room to actually respond to the kid in front of them. Elanza's own complaint, in her words to Stiaan, was sharp enough to become a running joke between them: she needed "a vervalste [fake] first aid certificate" just to get through the administrative hoops before she could get to the part of the job she was actually good at — being with children.

She is also, separately, already a small business owner. Elanza runs EM Muller Services, a police-clearance and fingerprinting business serving South African expats across New Zealand, live at sapspoliceclearance.com. She has already done the unglamorous parts of running a company in NZ — compliance, a live website, Google Business, client trust — years before PROSTARS existed. PROSTARS is not a first-time founder's leap of faith. It's an experienced educator and an experienced small-business owner pointing the same skill set at the thing she actually loves.

She is a mother of two — Mila and Lienka — which means every judgment call PROSTARS makes about a child's dignity, pace, and readiness gets tested against her own kitchen table first.

**The brand has to feel like her**: warm, direct, unimpressed by fluff, allergic to bureaucracy for its own sake, and completely serious about the idea that a child who feels safe and capable is worth more than a child who can do a perfect star jump. PROSTARS exists because Elanza already knows what the alternative looks like from the inside — and knows she can build something with more heart and less paperwork.

## 2. Brand Identity (summary — full detail in `BRAND_IDENTITY.md`)

**Mission:** To give every Canterbury child visible, provable proof that they are becoming more capable, more resilient, and more okay — one session at a time.

**Vision:** PROSTARS becomes the name Canterbury parents say the way earlier generations said Montessori or Kindygym — not a sports class, a category of childhood development that happens to move through play.

**Tagline direction (see `BRAND_IDENTITY.md` for full set):**
- *"Every Star Starts Somewhere."*
- *"Ready, Inside and Out."*
- *"Small Steps. Real Stars."*

**Values:** Presence over performance. Progress you can see. No child left behind the pace of the room. Adults earn trust by showing their work (to parents, in plain language).

## 3. Market Position

### Children (the participant)
- **Core age band, Phase 1:** 4–8 years (kindergarten to Year 3) — the age range where emotional-regulation and fundamental-movement gaps are most visible to parents and least served by existing Canterbury programmes.
- **Expansion age band, Phase 2+:** 9–12 years, once the method and coaching bench are proven.
- **Development stage need:** fundamental movement skills (balance, coordination, ball skills) bundled with nervous-system regulation, frustration tolerance, and social-cognitive skills (turn-taking, reading a peer's cues) — the stuff that normally gets siloed into "sport" on one side and "therapy" on the other.

### Parents (the buyer)
- **Problem:** New Zealand's own data shows 70% of parents rank screen time and its downstream effects (attention, mood, social skill) as their top parenting worry (nib NZ *State of the Nation* Parenting Survey), and Canterbury's child-psychology services carry long waitlists — internal research for this project put the figure at roughly half of NZ parents facing a wait for psychology support. Parents don't want a diagnosis. They want a weekly, affordable, physical, joyful thing that visibly moves the needle before a problem becomes a referral.
- **Desire:** legible proof of progress — not a participation certificate, a specific story they can tell at pickup ("she waited her turn three times today without being asked").
- **Buying reason:** PROSTARS sits in the pricing and positioning gap between a $12–18 sport class (fun, but no visible developmental throughline) and a $150–250 psychology session (effective, but clinical, waitlisted, and stigmatised). At **$22–25/session**, PROSTARS doesn't need parents to understand child-development pedagogy — they already feel the problem every morning at the school gate.

### Schools (the channel)
- Canterbury primary schools are actively supported by **Sport Canterbury** (Tū Manawa Active Aotearoa regional funding, Youth Activation Fund) to bring in outside providers who increase quality movement participation — this is an existing, funded on-ramp for exactly the kind of after-school or in-school programme PROSTARS is.
- The ask that unlocks everything, per Elanza's own plan: **one school principal who says yes** to a lunchtime slot, a hall, or an after-school block. That single relationship is the actual MVP — not a logo, not an app.
- Longer-term, schools are a channel for holiday programmes, in-class "readiness" workshops for whole classes (not just enrolled families), and staff-facing mini-training on the PROSTARS language of regulation (so teachers reinforce it during the school day).

## 4. Unique Differentiation — the PROSTARS METHOD™

### The brief for this framework
> Physical · Resilience · Opportunity · Social · Thinking · Achievement · Respect · Strength

### Evaluation
This is a solid *acrostic* — it spells PROSTARS cleanly, and every word is a legitimate child-development trait. But as a category-defining framework it has two problems:

1. **It reads like every other sports-values wheel.** "Physical, Respect, Achievement, Strength" is functionally the same list as the Proteus prototype's "Excellence, Teamwork, Effort, Growth," or Playball's own values pillars. It doesn't encode *why* PROSTARS is different — it just relabels the category PROSTARS is trying to leave.
2. **It has no internal structure.** Eight flat, equally-weighted words give a parent nothing to hold onto and give Elanza nothing to sequence a session around. Real frameworks that became categories (Montessori's planes of development, CASEL's five SEL competencies) have a shape, not just a list.

### The improvement: two layers, one acronym
Keep PROSTARS as the acrostic — it's ownable and it's literally the brand name — but split it into an **inner layer** (the actual pedagogy, the "Inner Readiness" IP Stiaan's research surfaced) and an **outer layer** (the visible, photographable, tell-your-partner-at-pickup proof that the inner layer is working). This directly encodes the founding insight: *"movement is one tool in the delivery, not the product. The product is a parent who can see their child becoming more okay, week by week."*

| Letter | Inner layer (what we actually teach) | Outer layer (what parents actually see) |
|---|---|---|
| **P** | **Presence** — settling the body and mind, arriving ready | |
| **R** | **Resilience** — recovering from a knock, a loss, a "no" | |
| **O** | **Observation** — reading your own state and a teammate's | |
| **S** | **Social Connection** — belonging, turn-taking, friendship skill | |
| **T** | **Trying** — the courage to attempt, effort over outcome | |
| **A** | | **Achievement** — badges, certificates, visible milestones |
| **R** | | **Respect** — for self, peers, rules — the social contract, on display |
| **S** | | **Strength** — physical competence, fundamental movement skills |

**Why this is better:** the first five letters (P-R-O-S-T) are the differentiated product — the thing a psychologist would recognise and a sports-only competitor doesn't teach. The last three (A-R-S) are the proof layer — the exact things a coach hands a parent at the door, a photo op, a sticker chart, a certificate. This is the structural difference between PROSTARS and a generic "eight values" wall poster: **every session is teaching the inner five, and generating evidence for the outer three.** A coach can plan a session by asking "which inner skill am I building today, and what outer proof will the parent get?" — that's usable pedagogy, not decoration.

This also gives Elanza language that is unmistakably hers to own, distinct from Playball's sport-skill vocabulary and distinct from clinical SEL jargon that would alienate a parent at a school gate.

## 5. Product Roadmap

### Phase 1 — Local Business MVP (Months 0–6, before any technology spend)
- Weekly after-school classes, one school hall, one age band (4–8), capped at 12–15 children per session at $22–25/session.
- Holiday programmes at the same venue during Canterbury school breaks (higher-margin, single-day or 3-day blocks).
- Birthday party "PROSTARS Experience" package — a natural word-of-mouth engine, priced at a premium versus weekly classes.
- A private parent WhatsApp/Facebook group per class cohort — the "parent community" the brief asks for, running on tools Elanza already knows, before any custom software exists.
- Manual, honest progress notes: one line per child per week, texted or WhatsApped to parents. This *is* the outer-layer proof loop, running on nothing but Elanza's attention, before software automates it.

### Phase 2 — Digital Platform (Months 6–18, once Phase 1 has paying, retained families)
A marketing + operations website. Full spec in `WEBSITE_BLUEPRINT.md`. Core features:
- Class discovery, term booking, and payment.
- Parent dashboard: which PROSTARS METHOD skills their child is building this term.
- Simple, non-clinical progress notes tied to the outer layer (Achievement / Respect / Strength) — never diagnostic language.
- Digital certificates and a photo/video gallery per class.
- Term newsletters and event announcements.

### Phase 3 — Mobile App (Months 18+, once there are enough coaches/classes to justify a build)
Full spec in `APP_ROADMAP.md`. Two experiences:
- **Child-facing:** low-stakes challenges, badges, and light game mechanics tied to the outer layer — never a leaderboard that could shame a slower-developing child.
- **Parent-facing:** progress, direct coach messaging, booking, and payments in one place.

**Sequencing discipline:** no phase starts until the previous one has real revenue and real retention. A polished app with zero enrolled children is worth nothing; a scrappy WhatsApp group with 20 retained families is a business.

## 6. Technical Recommendation

Keep the MVP boring on purpose.

- **Phase 1:** no custom software. A simple booking form (Google Forms/Fillout), payments via Stripe payment links or bank transfer, communication via WhatsApp/Facebook groups, a one-page marketing site (even a Carrd or Notion page is enough).
- **Phase 2:** **Next.js** (marketing site + booking + parent dashboard) on **Vercel**, with **Supabase** for auth, the Postgres database (families, children, enrolments, sessions, progress notes), and file storage (photos, certificates). This stack was chosen because it lets a solo/small team ship and iterate fast, has generous free tiers appropriate to a Canterbury-scale business, and gives a straight upgrade path to Phase 3 without a rewrite.
- **Phase 3:** a React Native (Expo) app sharing the same Supabase backend and API layer built for the Phase 2 dashboard — not a separate system. Push notifications via Expo's push service for coach-to-parent messages and session reminders.
- **Do not build:** a custom CMS, a native iOS/Android app in Phase 2, a franchise management portal, or a payments system before there is a second location. All of these are Phase 4+ problems, and premature investment here is the single biggest risk to this plan.

## 7. InsightForge Opportunity Assessment

| Dimension | Score /20 | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Market demand | 16/20 | Verified NZ-wide parent anxiety about screen time/attention (70% top concern) and a documented psychology-services waitlist; Canterbury specifically has active council/Sport Canterbury interest in youth movement participation. Demand signal is real but not yet validated with paying PROSTARS customers. |
| Competition | 14/20 | Crowded at the edges (gymnastics $7–15/session, coding $230–395/holiday block, sKids, Bricks4Kidz, Playball itself) but **no identified Canterbury competitor occupies the Inner Readiness positioning** — the gap between sport and clinical psychology is real and currently empty. |
| Uniqueness | 17/20 | The PROSTARS METHOD's two-layer structure (inner pedagogy / outer proof) is a genuinely defensible framework, not just a values poster — and it's grounded in the founder's authentic, first-hand critique of the incumbent (Playball), which is hard to copy. |
| Scalability | 13/20 | Coach-led, in-person delivery is inherently people-constrained; scaling requires a certification pipeline (see `FRANCHISE_MODEL.md`) which doesn't exist yet and depends entirely on Elanza's bandwidth to build it. |
| Franchise potential | 14/20 | Strong *if* Option B/C ambition is chosen (see below) — the method is teachable and the proof-layer format (badges/certificates) already licenses well. Requires disciplined documentation before any franchise conversation is credible. |
| Digital potential | 15/20 | Clean Phase 2/3 path on a modern, cheap stack; parent-progress-visibility features map directly onto the brand's core promise, so digital isn't bolted on — it's the proof engine. |

**Opportunity Score: 89/100**

This is a strong, fundable local business with a real (not decorative) point of differentiation, and a credible — but not guaranteed — path to a regional or national brand. The single biggest risk is not market or product: it's execution bandwidth. The strategy has existed since June 2026 and stalled on the founder's time, not on the idea's quality. Everything in `90_DAY_EXECUTION_PLAN.md` is designed around that constraint first.
