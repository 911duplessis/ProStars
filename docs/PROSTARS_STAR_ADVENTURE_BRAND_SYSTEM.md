# PROSTARS™ — Star Adventure Brand System

**Founder decision, locked:** ⭐ The Star Adventure.
Her words, in full: *"Star een."*

This document refines that choice into the real brand system — it does not introduce new concepts. Where earlier documents (`PROSTARS_TEACHER_LAUNCH_KIT.md`, `BRAND_IDENTITY.md`) already got something right, this doc keeps it. Where her feedback revealed a gap, this doc fills it. The one thing that changes everything else: her comment on the three worlds.

> *"Dit lyk vir my soos kleintjies... dan 3 jariges dan 4-5 jariges."*
> (This looks like little ones to me... then 3-year-olds, then 4-5-year-olds.)

She wasn't rejecting anything. She was doing her actual job: reading a room of children by age, instinctively, the way a kindergarten teacher does. She looked at one static image and saw a developmental sequence in it. That instinct is now the spine of this whole system — **the brand grows up alongside the child**, instead of staying one fixed image for ages 3 through 8.

---

## 1. Final Brand Personality

Locked, not revisited:

- **Warm** — a trusted adult's warmth, not a mascot's forced cheerfulness.
- **Playful** — delivered through movement and story, never through decoration for its own sake.
- **Premium** — calm, considered, Montessori-adjacent trust — not loud, not primary-colour-cluttered.
- **Trusted by parents** — every visible thing (medal, certificate, shirt) exists to prove something real happened, not to look nice.

**It must never look like:** a sports academy, Playball, a school worksheet, a generic cartoon daycare.
**It must feel like:** *every child discovers their own star.*

The tagline is unchanged and confirmed: **"Every Star Starts Somewhere."**

---

## 2. Logo Refinement Directions

The mark stays: the **Open Star** — a five-point star with one point deliberately left unclosed. What refines now is *how it's used*, not what it is.

- **Primary lock-up:** Open Star + "PROSTARS™" wordmark, Baloo 2 (or equivalent rounded, friendly display face), set tight beside or under the mark.
- **New refinement — the mark now has a job beyond the logo.** The exact same star outline is the base unit of the **Star Journey system** (§5): drawn 25% complete, 55%, 82%, then fully closed and filled gold. A designer taking this to production needs to build the star as one clean vector path so it can be partially stroked accurately at each stage — not as four separate hand-drawn stars. See `docs/star-journey/index.html` for the working mechanic.
- **Three lock-up variants to produce:**
  1. Full colour, coral stroke, open point — everyday use (pamphlets, signage, the everyday t-shirt).
  2. Single colour black — certificates, stamps, anything printed cheaply at home.
  3. Solid gold, fully closed — reserved *only* for PROSTAR-tier (age 8) materials and the completion medal. Never used as the default logo. Closing the star is earned, not decorative.
- **Sizes to test:** 2cm badge (open-point version must still read clearly at this size — if it doesn't, the point-gap may need to be slightly wider in the small-format version), t-shirt chest print, banner.

## 3. Mascot Development — Pip

Pip stays exactly as introduced: a young star who hasn't grown their last point yet.

**What refines:** Pip is now explicitly positioned as the **Growing Star** (ages 4–5) character — Pip's own stage. This resolves a question the three-worlds comparison left open (what is Pip's age?) and gives teachers a concrete answer when a child asks.

- Pip does not get four different redesigns for four age tiers — that's more character development than a single mascot needs, and it dilutes the "one steady, familiar face" principle from the Teacher Launch Kit. Instead:
  - For **Little Star** (age 3) sessions, Pip is introduced more simply — fewer words, shorter story beats, Pip as a picture more than a narrator.
  - For **Bright Star** and **PROSTAR** (ages 6–8) sessions, Pip can "look up to" the child a little — framed as slightly younger, so an 8-year-old feels like the more capable one in the relationship, not like they've outgrown a character aimed at toddlers.
- Pip's core story line — *"I'm a star who's still growing my last point, just like you"* — now pays off concretely: it's not just a nice line, it's literally the mechanic every child lives through on their own Star Journey badge.

## 4. Age Positioning

This is the section her feedback actually asked for. Three bands, each with its own delivery adjustments — same 8-week Confidence Adventure structure and PROSTARS METHOD underneath, different execution.

### Age 3 — Little Star
- **Session length:** shorter — 30 minutes, not 45. Attention span first, content second.
- **Group size:** smaller ratio, more hands-on adult support per child.
- **Language:** single-idea sessions ("today we try"), minimal explanation, mostly demonstration and repetition.
- **Visuals:** the simplest, roundest version of everything — big shapes, few colours per scene, Pip present but quiet.
- **Parent message:** "Your child is safe, seen, and having fun" — not yet "your child is developing resilience." Keep it concrete and sensory.

### Ages 4–5 — Growing Star
- **Session length:** the standard 45 minutes, as already built in the Teacher Launch Kit.
- **This is the default design target** — the existing 8-week programme, session structure, and Pip's storytelling were all built around this band. No changes needed here; it's the centre of gravity.
- **Parent message:** the full PROSTARS promise as already written in the parent pamphlet — weekly proof, visible growth.

### Ages 6–7 — Bright Star
- **Session length:** 45–50 minutes, with more time in the Team Activity block — this age band can sustain longer cooperative games and starts caring about fairness and rules.
- **Language:** can handle the "why," not just the "what" — a Bright Star session can name the feeling ("that's resilience — bouncing back after a wobble") where a Little Star session just lives it.
- **Role shift:** children this age can be given small leadership moments within the session (leading the Star Circle greeting, choosing the next game) — this is where "I Can Lead" (Week 7) really lands.
- **Parent message:** shift toward independence and confidence language — "she's starting to lead, not just follow."

### Age 8 — PROSTAR
- **Session length:** up to 60 minutes, more complex problem-solving and team challenges.
- **Role:** informal peer-mentor role toward younger children during shared events (holiday programmes, open days) — an 8-year-old PROSTAR helping a 3-year-old Little Star find their name tag is a real, low-cost way to make the age system visible and meaningful in the room, not just on paper.
- **Parent message:** this is the "graduation" framing — the completed star, the full journey, the strongest re-enrolment/testimonial moment PROSTARS has.

## 5. The Star Journey System

**Working visual:** `docs/star-journey/index.html`

Four tiers, one continuous mark:

| Tier | Age | Star state | One-line meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🌱 **Little Star** | 3 | 25% drawn | Just arriving, just belonging |
| ⭐ **Growing Star** | 4–5 | 55% drawn | Trying, playing, building the five inner skills (Pip's own stage) |
| ✨ **Bright Star** | 6–7 | 82% drawn | More independence, more leadership |
| 🌟 **PROSTAR** | 8 | Fully closed, solid gold | The journey, complete |

**How this relates to the 8-week programme (unchanged):** every term, at any age, still runs the same PROSTARS 8-Week Confidence Adventure — Week 1 "I Belong" through Week 8 "I Am A PROSTAR" — exactly as built in the Teacher Launch Kit. The Star Journey is not a replacement for that; it's the layer above it. A 4-year-old's Week 8 medal and certificate now visually carry the **Growing Star** badge; an 8-year-old's Week 8 medal and certificate carry the completed **PROSTAR** badge. Same ceremony, same weekly stickers, same "I Am A PROSTAR" language at the end of every single term — the art just grows up with the child across years.

This is also the answer to the founder's "she needs the brand to grow with the child" instinct: a parent who enrols a 3-year-old can now see, literally, a five-year path ahead — not just one term.

## 6. Badge / Medal Progression

Builds directly on the Teacher Launch Kit's existing medal design (§5 there) — this doesn't replace it, it adds a tier layer.

- **Weekly stickers within a term:** unchanged — one sticker per week, building up the child's own star chart, as already specified.
- **End-of-term medal:** same laminated-card-to-start / metal-later production approach already specified — but the star artwork on the medal now reflects the child's current Star Journey tier (25% / 55% / 82% / complete), not one universal design.
- **The completed PROSTAR medal only exists at age 8.** Nothing before that point should use a fully closed, solid-gold star — that exclusivity is what makes reaching it mean something. This is a hard rule for whoever produces the physical medals: closed-and-gold is earned once, not printed for everyone.
- **Ribbon colour stays constant** (indigo with a coral stripe) across all four tiers — the *star* shows progress; the ribbon shows it's still PROSTARS.

## 7. Certificate System

Refines the Teacher Launch Kit's certificate (§6 there): same A4 landscape layout, same warm sand background and indigo border, same Pip-in-the-corner — but the wording and star-watermark state now match the child's tier:

| Tier | Certificate line |
|---|---|
| Little Star | "Has taken her first steps as a Little Star." |
| Growing Star | "Has grown so much this term as a Growing Star." |
| Bright Star | "Is shining bright as a confident Bright Star." |
| PROSTAR | "Is officially a PROSTAR." |

The watermark star behind the text is drawn at the matching completion percentage. Everything else — the row of 8 week-titles with ticks, the date, "Your PROSTARS Coach" signature line (no name, per the naming note) — stays exactly as already specified.

## 8. T-Shirt Designs

The two-shirt system from the Teacher Launch Kit stays (an everyday tee + a completion tee) — no new garments needed. What refines:

- **Everyday tee:** unchanged — sand/cream, small Open Star chest print, Pip on the sleeve or collar. Worn by every child, every age, every week.
- **Completion tee:** the back-print star graphic now matches the child's current tier completion percentage instead of always showing "I AM A PROSTAR" — a 4-year-old finishing their term gets a shirt with their 55%-drawn Growing Star and "GROWING STAR" text; only an 8-year-old's shirt says "I AM A PROSTAR" with the fully closed star.
- This turns the shirt itself into a visible, wearable marker of age-stage — exactly the "grows with the child" read Lal had on first seeing the concepts.

## 10. Classroom / Session Visuals

Builds on the Teacher Launch Kit's five-part session structure (Welcome Activity, Movement/Play, Inner Readiness Lesson, Team Activity, Achievement Moment) — same shape every week, every age, as already specified. What's new here is age-band execution:

- **A single portable "Star Journey" chart or banner**, showing all four tiers, displayed at every session regardless of which age band is in the room — so a Little Star can see what's ahead, and a Bright Star can see how far they've come. One object, works for every class.
- **Per-age adjustments to the existing structure:**
  - Little Star (3): shorter blocks within the 30-minute session, more repetition, less sitting-still time in the Inner Readiness Lesson.
  - Growing Star (4–5): the structure exactly as built in the Teacher Launch Kit.
  - Bright Star (6–7): longer Team Activity block, occasional child-led Welcome Activity.
  - PROSTAR (8): optional peer-mentor role during shared/mixed-age events only — not a change to their own weekly session structure.
- **Parent-facing pickup corner:** unchanged from the exploration doc's suggestion — a clipboard or small board showing the week's theme and current tier, doing quiet marketing work in the room itself.
